Binding tier

Once you sign in, the plugin binds your session to a device-resident key. The tier shown here tells you which kind.

What it defends against

Only the routes you put dbsc.requireProof() in front of demand the proof header. Here that's /api/profile and /api/payment. Your real app picks its own list (payments, admin actions, password or email change, 2FA enroll). Unguarded routes like /api/me accept the bare cookie like any normal app.

allow The legit request

Your bound device signs a per-request proof. requireProof() verifies it, then the route runs.

deny The stolen cookie

An attacker exfiltrates your session cookie and pastes it into their browser. They have the cookie. They don't have the key.

deny The tampered body

An MITM sees you sign {amount: 1} and tries to rewrite it to {amount: 1000} mid-flight. The proof signs the body hash too.
